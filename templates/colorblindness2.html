Color Blindness Detection

"Color blindness, scientifically referred to as color vision deficiency, is a visual impairment stemming from the malfunction or absence of specific photoreceptor cells in the eye. This condition hinders accurate perception of certain colors, particularly red and green, impacting various aspects of daily life.
The Ishihara color blindness test stands as a widely accepted diagnostic tool, employing specially designed plates with intricate patterns of differently colored dots. These patterns are used to identify and categorize various forms of color blindness, aiding in personalized accommodation strategies for individuals in fields such as design, accessibility, and healthcare."
